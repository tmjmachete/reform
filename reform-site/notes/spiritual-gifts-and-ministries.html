17
The Church

Spiritual Gifts & Ministries

← Back to all beliefs

Every member of the church has been given a spiritual gift. Not some members — every member. These gifts are not ornaments for personal display but tools for service, given by the Spirit for the common good of the body and the advancement of the gospel.

Given by the Spirit

Spiritual gifts are not natural talents polished by effort — though talents can become instruments of the Spirit. They are supernatural endowments given by the Holy Spirit as He wills, to equip the church for its divinely ordained mission. No believer is ungifted, and no gift is unimportant. The body needs every part.

1 Corinthians 12:7 "Now to each one the manifestation of the Spirit is given for the common good."

Diverse but United

The gifts are diverse — faith, healing, prophecy, teaching, service, administration, mercy, and more. But they all come from the same Spirit, are given for the same purpose, and are exercised toward the same goal: the building up of the body of Christ to spiritual maturity. Diversity of gifts produces unity of mission.

Ephesians 4:11–12 "So Christ himself gave the apostles, the prophets, the evangelists, the pastors and teachers, to equip his people for works of service, so that the body of Christ may be built up."

Faithful Stewardship

Gifts received must be gifts employed. To receive a spiritual gift and leave it unused is to misunderstand the purpose of grace. We are stewards of what the Spirit has entrusted to us — and stewards are expected to invest, not merely preserve. As we faithfully use our gifts, the church grows and the world sees Christ.

1 Peter 4:10 "Each of you should use whatever gift you have received to serve others, as faithful stewards of God's grace in its various forms."
