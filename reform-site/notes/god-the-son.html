God the Son

← Back to all beliefs

In Jesus Christ, God became one of us — not as a performance or a visit, but as a permanent commitment. The eternal Son took on human flesh, lived the life we should have lived, died the death we deserved, and rose to make intercession for us still. He is our Saviour, our Lord, and our coming King.

Fully God, Fully Human

The incarnation is the most staggering event in the history of the universe. The One through whom all things were created entered his own creation as a creature. He was not pretending to be human — he was truly human, facing real temptation, experiencing real hunger, grief, and exhaustion, yet without sin. In him, divinity and humanity are permanently joined.

John 1:14 "The Word became flesh and made his dwelling among us. We have seen his glory, the glory of the one and only Son, who came from the Father, full of grace and truth."

His Life, Death, and Resurrection

Christ's life was not merely preparation for the cross — it was itself an act of salvation. His perfect obedience fulfilled what we could not. His death on the cross bore the full penalty for our sin. His resurrection declared victory over death and guaranteed our own. He now ministers in the heavenly sanctuary as our High Priest, interceding for us at the right hand of the Father.

Hebrews 4:15–16 "For we do not have a high priest who is unable to feel sympathy for our weaknesses, but we have one who has been tempted in every way, just as we are — yet he did not sin. Let us then approach God's throne of grace with confidence."

He Is Coming Again

The story of the Son is not finished. The same Jesus who ascended into heaven will return — visibly, personally, and gloriously — to deliver his people and restore all things. This is not wishful thinking. It is the certain promise of the One who has never broken his word.

Acts 1:11 "This same Jesus, who has been taken from you into heaven, will come back in the same way you have seen him go into heaven."
