How Do I Get There?

You and I need to find our way back. Our lives depend on it.

David asks in Psalm 119:9 — “Wherewithal shall a young man cleanse his way?” It is the question of someone who already knows the answer should be God, but cannot figure out how to get from where he is to where God is.

Paul’s cry in Romans 7 is the cry of someone who has been living in that gap for too long: “Who shall deliver me from this body of death?” This is not the language of a backslider who does not care. This is the language of one who is exhausted. Tired of being his own enemy. Tired of always being undone by the same thing. Tired of losing to himself.

If that is you, stay with me.

The Answer Is Not More Effort

The Answer Is Not More Effort

The natural response to losing is to try harder. To make more resolutions. To draw a new line and dare yourself not to cross it. But that is the same strategy that has been failing you. The problem is not that you have not tried. The problem is what you are trying with.

Ministry of Healing, p. 176

“We cannot change our hearts, we cannot control our thoughts, our impulses, our affections. We cannot make ourselves pure, fit for God’s service. But we can choose to serve God, we can give Him our will; then He will work in us to will and to do according to His good pleasure. Thus our whole nature will be brought under the control of Christ. Through the right exercise of the will, an entire change may be made in the life. By yielding up the will to Christ, we ally ourselves with divine power.”

Philippians 2:13 “For it is God which worketh in you both to will and to do of his good pleasure.”

The will is not the engine. It is the steering wheel. You cannot generate the power. But you can point yourself toward the One who can. This is the distinction that changes everything. Not willpower. Surrendered will.

“Satan trembles and flees before the weakest soul who finds refuge in that mighty name.” — The Great Controversy, p. 530

The Power of Belief

The Power of Belief

Scientists have documented something remarkable about the placebo effect. A patient given a sugar pill — told it is medicine — will sometimes experience measurable, physical improvement. The body responds to what the mind genuinely believes. Belief is not merely psychological. It is physiological. It changes things.

Now consider what genuine faith in a living God does to the soul.

Not the faith that says “I hope this works.” The faith that says “I know who He is and I know what He has done and I am placing everything I have on that fact.” That kind of faith does not produce placebo results. It produces resurrection.

The placebo works because the patient believes the treatment is real. The treatment is real. The Physician is real. And He has never lost a patient who came to Him.

The Enemy Is Closer Than You Think

The Enemy Is Closer Than You Think

Here is the harder truth. We are quick to blame the devil for our failures. And he is real, and he is active. But James will not let us hide behind him:

James 1:13–16 “Let no man say when he is tempted, I am tempted of God: for God cannot be tempted with evil, neither tempteth he any man: but every man is tempted, when he is drawn away of his own lust, and enticed. Then when lust hath conceived, it bringeth forth sin: and sin, when it is finished, bringeth forth death. Do not err, my beloved brethren.”

The devil cannot force you to do his bidding if you choose not to. He has no power that you do not hand him. The chain begins inside — with desire, with the thing you have not yet laid down, with the idleness you have not yet refused. The devil walks through open doors. Stop leaving them open.

Last Day Events, p. 67.2

“Build a wall of scriptures around you, and you will see that the world cannot break it down. Commit the Scriptures to memory, and then throw right back upon Satan when he comes with his temptations, ‘It is written.’ This is the way that our Lord met the temptations of Satan, and resisted them.”

Dead in Trespasses

Dead in Trespasses

But what if the door has been open so long you no longer know how to close it? What if the habit is so deep, the pattern so entrenched, that the language of choice feels like mockery?

Then you need more than a decision. You need a resurrection.

Jesus stood outside the tomb of Lazarus — four days dead, already decomposing — and called him by name. Lazarus did not muster the strength to respond. He did not will himself back to life. He was dead. He had no capacity to cooperate, no will to exercise, no strength to rise. And yet at the voice of Christ, he came out.

John 11:25 “I am the resurrection, and the life: he that believeth in me, though he were dead, yet shall he live.”

Paul tells us in Ephesians 2 that this is not merely a story about Lazarus. It is our story. “You hath he quickened, who were dead in trespasses and sins.” And then verse 5, so that we do not miss it: “Even when we were dead in sins, hath quickened us together with Christ.”

He did not help us get up. He raised us.

If you feel too far gone — too deep in, too long gone, too thoroughly defeated — you are not beyond the reach of the voice that called Lazarus out of a sealed tomb. All Lazarus did was come out when called. Come out.

You Will Not Always Feel It Working

You Will Not Always Feel It Working

John 3:8 “The wind bloweth where it listeth, and thou hearest the sound thereof, but canst not tell whence it cometh, and whither it goeth: so is every one that is born of the Spirit.”

The Spirit moves. You will not always see it. You will not always feel it. There will be weeks of faithfulness with no visible result — no dramatic breakthrough, no sudden lightness, no feeling of victory. You will wonder if anything is happening at all.

And then one day, quietly, you will notice something. The thing that used to own you no longer has the same grip. The temptation that always won is losing. You said no where before you never could. You did not manufacture that. You did not even notice it growing. The wind was blowing the whole time.

Ministry of Healing, p. 452

“Wrongs cannot be righted, nor can reformations in conduct be made by a few feeble, intermittent efforts. Character building is the work, not of a day, nor of a year, but of a lifetime.”

A lifetime. Not a week of devotions. Not a season of commitment. A lifetime of daily choices, each one small, each one pointed in the same direction. The road home is long. But it is a road. And every step taken in the right direction is a step further from where you were.

Seek Him Through the Word

It can seem like the common thing to say — read the Word of God. And perhaps you have heard it so many times it has lost its weight. So consider this:

God said “Let there be light” — and light came into a darkened world. Three words. The darkness did not negotiate. It did not resist. It fled. That same word, the living Word, is the instrument He has placed in your hands. If a let there be could bring light to a universe, think about what you taking heed to that word can do to your life.

The next verse of Psalm 119 answers David’s question before he even finishes asking it: “With my whole heart have I sought thee: O let me not wander from thy commandments.”

The whole heart. Not the convenient heart. Not the Sabbath morning heart. The whole heart, brought to the pages of Holy Writ, seeking the God who is already seeking you.

“With my whole heart have I sought thee: O let me not wander from thy commandments.” — Psalm 119:10

David asked how a young man cleanses his way. He answered his own question: “by taking heed thereto according to thy word.”

The Word. The will surrendered. The faith that does not wait to feel something before it acts. The long road walked one day at a time, by a God who is doing more in you than you can see.

You can get there. Not because you are strong enough. Because He is.
