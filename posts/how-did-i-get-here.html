Issue No. 02  ·  The Descent

How Did I Get Here?

Reform Journal  ·  May 2026

Last time we stood at the mirror. Today we ask the harder question — not just what we see, but how we arrived here.

How did I get here? How have I become so vile in my thinking? How have I become a friend of misery and depression?

The Great Controversy, p. 49 "Little by little, at first in stealth and silence, and then more openly as it increased in strength and gained control of the minds of men, 'the mystery of iniquity' carried forward its deceptive and blasphemous work."

The person at the bottom of that basement did not arrive there in one leap. There are stairs — individual steps — and for this person to get here, each one had to be taken, descending one at a time toward the bottom.

Mind, Character and Personality, Vol. 1, p. 75 "The mind does not come down at once from purity and holiness to depravity, corruption, and crime. It takes time to degrade those formed in the image of God to the brutal or the satanic. By beholding we become changed. By the indulgence of impure thoughts man can so educate his mind that sin which he once loathed will become pleasant to him."
The Steps

What are the steps that we take? The story of King David in 2 Samuel 11 maps them with uncomfortable precision.

2 Samuel 11:1–2 (KJV) "And it came to pass, after the year was expired, at the time when kings go forth to battle, that David sent Joab, and his servants with him, and all Israel; and they destroyed the children of Ammon, and besieged Rabbah. But David tarried still at Jerusalem. And it came to pass in an eveningtide, that David arose from off his bed, and walked upon the roof of the king's house: and from the roof he saw a woman washing herself; and the woman was very beautiful to look upon."

Every descent begins with a single step.

01
Idleness
At a time when kings were expected to go to war, David decided to remain behind. That decision opened up an opportunity which led to his demise. The same applies today — when there is nothing productive for us to do with our time, the devil stands knocking. The old adage is right: an idle mind is the devil's playground.
02
Desire
David does not merely notice that Bathsheba is bathing — the verse tells us "she was very beautiful to look upon." This does not happen at a glance. Our mother Eve looked at the tree and saw that it was good for food. Would this occur at a glance? No. She had to look and desire. The same thing happens with David. Desire requires attention, and attention requires time.
03
Action
David follows a detailed sequence — he asks who the woman is, she is called for, and finally they are together. When we are desirous of what is sinful and harmful, we adequately plan a time, a place, and a course of action. The planning itself is already the sin in motion.
The Domino Effect

One falls. Then all the others follow.

Sinning can be likened to the domino effect. One domino sets all the others in motion — similar to how this person took one step at a time to arrive here. We are where we are because of small decisions that have compounded over time.

The Way Back

But here is the good news — if we got here by steps, there is a way back by steps. And God has not left us without direction.

Jeremiah 6:16 (KJV) "Thus saith the LORD, Stand ye in the ways, and see, and ask for the old paths, where is the good way, and walk therein, and ye shall find rest for your souls."

It is time to seek and ask for the old paths. Ellen White writes in Ministry of Healing: "He traces from cause to effect." It is time for us to do the same — to trace, through the Word, how we arrived here, and how we find our way back.

The Word That Guides Us Back
"Through thy precepts I get understanding: therefore I hate every false way."
Psalm 119:104 (KJV)

Understanding comes through the precepts of God. Not through willpower alone, not through shame, not through our own effort to retrace the steps — but through the Word. The same Word that shows us how we fell is the Word that shows us the way home.

Until Next Time
Keep walking. The old paths are still there — and they still lead home.
