Death is not what the world thinks it is. It is not the doorway to another conscious existence — it is a sleep, an unconscious waiting, from which every person will be awakened at the resurrection. This truth is not a diminishment of hope. It magnifies it. All our hope is concentrated in Christ and in the resurrection morning that is coming.

Death Is a Sleep

The consistent witness of Scripture is that the dead do not continue in conscious existence. They sleep — without awareness, without suffering, without experience of the passage of time. The righteous dead are not yet in heaven. They are resting, safely held in the memory and purpose of God, waiting for the resurrection that Christ has guaranteed.

Ecclesiastes 9:5 "For the living know that they will die, but the dead know nothing; they have no further reward, and even their name is forgotten."

The Resurrection of the Righteous

At the second coming of Christ, the righteous dead will be raised — bodily, gloriously, permanently. This is not the immortal soul escaping the body. This is the whole person, restored and transformed, reunited with Christ forever. The resurrection is the great reversal: the last enemy, death, finally destroyed.

1 Thessalonians 4:16–17 "For the Lord himself will come down from heaven, with a loud command, with the voice of the archangel and with the trumpet call of God, and the dead in Christ will rise first. After that, we who are still alive and are left will be caught up together with them in the clouds to meet the Lord in the air."

Why This Truth Matters

The biblical teaching on death is not merely academic — it has profound practical implications. It exposes the deceptions of spiritualism, which claims to communicate with the dead. It protects us from teachings that place the departed in a state of conscious suffering or reward before the judgment. And it focuses all our hope where it belongs: on Christ, on the resurrection, and on the life of the age to come.

John 11:25 "Jesus said to her, 'I am the resurrection and the life. The one who believes in me will live, even though they die.'"
