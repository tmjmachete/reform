God the Father is not a distant sovereign. He is the Creator who knows what he made, the Sustainer who holds it together, and the Father who has pledged his own personal presence to guide us home. He is near — closer than we dare to believe.

Creator and Sovereign

Everything that exists owes its existence to the Father. He did not merely set the universe in motion and step back — He sustains it moment by moment by the word of his power. The same God who spoke galaxies into existence is the God who knows the number of hairs on your head and the burdens you carry today.

Genesis 1:1 "In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth."

His Character

The Father is just and holy, yet merciful and gracious. He is slow to anger and abounding in steadfast love. These are not competing attributes — they are the unified character of a God who hates sin because he loves the sinner. His justice and his mercy are not in tension; they meet at the cross.

Exodus 34:6–7 "The LORD, the LORD, the compassionate and gracious God, slow to anger, abounding in love and faithfulness, maintaining love to thousands, and forgiving wickedness, rebellion and sin."

What Nation Has God So Near?

Moses asked a question that still echoes through time: what great nation has its god near to them the way the LORD our God is near to us? (Deuteronomy 4:7). The Father is not unapproachable. Through Christ, access to the Father is fully and freely granted — and we, who were strangers, have been brought near as his own children.

1 John 3:1 "See what great love the Father has lavished on us, that we should be called children of God! And that is what we are!"
