Salvation is not a transaction we initiate — it is a rescue God accomplishes. Led by the Holy Spirit, we sense our need, confess our sin, and exercise faith in Jesus as Saviour and Lord. What follows is not merely forgiveness. It is a complete transformation of who we are.

Grace Alone, Through Faith Alone

We bring nothing to our salvation but our need. God brings everything — the righteousness of Christ, the conviction of the Spirit, and the gift of faith itself. Salvation is not earned by works, not maintained by performance, and not lost by failure alone. It is received by faith and sustained by grace, which is always greater than our sin.

Ephesians 2:8–9 "For it is by grace you have been saved, through faith — and this is not from yourselves, it is the gift of God — not by works, so that no one can boast."

Justified, Adopted, Transformed

Through Christ we are justified — declared righteous before God. We are adopted — received as sons and daughters into the family of God. And through the Spirit we are sanctified — progressively transformed into the likeness of Christ. These are not three separate experiences but three dimensions of one salvation.

Romans 8:1 "Therefore, there is now no condemnation for those who are in Christ Jesus."

Assurance and Obedience

The fruit of salvation is obedience — not as a condition for salvation but as evidence of it. Those who have truly received the grace of God find that they want to live differently. Love for God becomes the motivation for keeping his commandments, and the Spirit provides the power to do what the law requires.

John 14:15 "If you love me, keep my commands."
