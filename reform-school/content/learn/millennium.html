The Motives of Repentance

This moment raises an important question: what are my motives behind repentance? Am I repentant because I recognise the sinfulness of sin — or am I lamenting that I have been caught?

Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 181"Esau was not shut out from the privilege of seeking God's favor by repentance, but he could find no means of recovering the birthright. His grief did not spring from conviction of sin; he did not desire to be reconciled to God. He sorrowed because of the results of his sin, but not for the sin itself."

The Responsibility of Leadership

Ezekiel 9:1–6Judgment begins at the sanctuary — at the house of God.

If you are not leading the flock toward eternity, you are leading them toward damnation. This is not just for pastors — it applies to every husband, wife, parent, and friend.
