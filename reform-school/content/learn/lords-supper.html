The Lord's Supper is not a memorial to a dead hero. It is a communion with a living Saviour — present to meet, to strengthen, and to remind us of what he has done and what he is coming to do. Every time we take the bread and the cup, we proclaim the Lord's death until he comes.

A Participation in Christ

The bread and the cup are symbols — but symbols of the most significant realities in the universe. The bread represents the body of Christ, broken for us. The cup represents his blood, poured out for the forgiveness of sins. To receive them in faith is to receive Christ himself — his sacrifice, his righteousness, his life given for ours.

1 Corinthians 11:26 "For whenever you eat this bread and drink this cup, you proclaim the Lord's death until he comes."

Preparation and Examination

The Lord's Supper calls us to examine ourselves — not to achieve a level of perfection before we can participate, but to come honestly, acknowledging our need and our dependence on the One who is the only source of life. The service of foot-washing that precedes it is a profound act of humility, renewing our willingness to serve as Christ served.

John 13:14–15 "Now that I, your Lord and Teacher, have washed your feet, you also should wash one another's feet. I have set you an example that you should do as I have done for you."

Open to All Who Believe

The communion service is open to all who believe in Christ and have examined their own hearts. It is not the property of any denomination or tradition — it belongs to every child of God who comes in faith, remembering what Christ has done, and longing for the day when he comes again.

Luke 22:19 "And he took bread, gave thanks and broke it, and gave it to them, saying, 'This is my body given for you; do this in remembrance of me.'"
