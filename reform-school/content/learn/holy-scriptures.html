The Bible is not merely an ancient book of wisdom. It is the written Word of God — given by divine inspiration, authoritative in all it teaches, and sufficient for everything we need to know about salvation, character, and the will of God.

Given by Inspiration

The Scriptures did not originate with human minds. Holy men and women spoke and wrote as they were moved by the Holy Spirit. This does not mean God dictated every word mechanically — it means He worked through the personalities, languages, and circumstances of the writers to communicate exactly what He intended. The result is a Word that is both fully human and fully divine.

2 Timothy 3:16–17 "All Scripture is God-breathed and is useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting and training in righteousness, so that the servant of God may be thoroughly equipped for every good work."

Supreme and Sufficient

The Bible is the supreme authority for the church — not tradition, not human reason, not personal experience. These may serve as helpful companions to Scripture, but they are never its equal and never its judge. Scripture judges them. The Word of God is the standard of character, the test of experience, and the definitive revealer of doctrine.

Isaiah 8:20 "Consult God's instruction and the testimony of warning. If anyone does not speak according to this word, they have no light of dawn."

Living and Active

The Bible is not a relic. It is a living document that the Holy Spirit uses to speak to every generation with fresh urgency. When we open it, we are not merely reading history — we are entering into a conversation with the God who still speaks, still calls, and still transforms.

Hebrews 4:12 "For the word of God is alive and active. Sharper than any double-edged sword, it penetrates even to dividing soul and spirit, joints and marrow; it judges the thoughts and attitudes of the heart."
