We are not accidents of time and matter. We are not autonomous gods. We are image-bearers — created with dignity, marred by sin, and being restored by grace. To understand what we are is to understand both the depth of our need and the height of our hope.

Made in the Image of God

The creation of humanity is the crown of the creation week. God did not speak us into existence from a distance — He formed us, breathed into us, and declared us very good. To bear the image of God is to reflect his character: love, reason, moral freedom, and the capacity for relationship. We are not divine, but we are uniquely and permanently his.

Genesis 1:26–27 "Then God said, 'Let us make mankind in our image, in our likeness'... So God created mankind in his own image, in the image of God he created them; male and female he created them."

An Indivisible Unity

Scripture presents the human person as a unified whole — body, mind, and spirit are not separate competing parts but dimensions of a single life. We do not have souls; we are souls. Death, therefore, is not the liberation of an immortal soul from the body — it is the cessation of life in its entirety, awaiting the resurrection that God has promised.

Genesis 2:7 "Then the LORD God formed a man from the dust of the ground and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life, and the man became a living being."

Fallen but Not Forsaken

When our first parents chose independence over trust, the image of God in them was marred. We inherited their fallen nature — weaknesses and tendencies toward self, toward sin, toward death. But God did not abandon his image-bearers. In Christ, the restoration has already begun. The Spirit renews our minds, grace restores the image, and the resurrection will complete the work that God set in motion before the foundation of the world.

2 Corinthians 3:18 "And we all, who with unveiled faces contemplate the Lord's glory, are being transformed into his image with ever-increasing glory, which comes from the Lord, who is the Spirit."
