There is one God — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. Not three separate gods, nor three names for one shapeshifting being, but three coeternal Persons in perfect, loving unity. This is not a puzzle to be solved but a reality to be known.

One God, Three Persons

The doctrine of the Trinity does not originate with church councils — it emerges from the Scriptures themselves. From the plural pronouns of Genesis to the triadic blessing of the New Testament letters, the Bible consistently reveals a God who is both one and three: unified in nature, distinct in Person, united in purpose.

Genesis 1:26 "Then God said, 'Let us make mankind in our image, in our likeness.'"

United in Love

The unity of the Trinity is not an administrative arrangement — it is a unity of love. The Father, Son, and Holy Spirit exist in an eternal fellowship of giving and receiving. This is why John could write that God does not merely have love, but is love. Love requires relationship, and the Trinity is that relationship in its eternal and perfect form.

1 John 4:8 "Whoever does not love does not know God, because God is love."

Why It Matters

The Trinity is the ground of our salvation. The Father sends, the Son redeems, the Spirit renews. Each Person is fully God, each plays a distinct role in our rescue, and together they accomplish what none could do alone — the full restoration of fallen humanity into the image and fellowship of a loving God.

Matthew 28:19 "Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptising them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit."
