The history of the great conflict between good and evil — from the time it first began in heaven to the final overthrow of rebellion — is a demonstration of God's unchanging love.

The Origin of Evil

Tradition and misinterpretation have distorted the definition of sin. To understand sin rightly, we must first understand the character of God — and God's character is love.

Patriarchs and Prophets, p. 33"The law of love being the foundation of the government of God, the happiness of all created beings depended upon their perfect accord with its great principles of righteousness."

God is defined as One — one in nature, one in character, one in purpose. That unity is held together by love. His law is an expression of himself. To remove the law is to remove his identity, and that identity is love.

The Three Traits of Lucifer's Fall

Three traits marked the beginning of the controversy — envy, jealousy, and pride. Little by little, Lucifer came to indulge the desire for self-exaltation.

Great Controversy, p. 493"He takes no pleasure in a forced allegiance, and to all He grants freedom of will, that they may render Him voluntary service."

Lucifer claimed that God's service was forced. But God's delayed punishment was not weakness — it was a sign of him allowing sin to fully reveal itself for what it truly is.

God Has Always Been With Us
