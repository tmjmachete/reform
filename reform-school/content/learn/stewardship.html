Everything we have — time, talent, money, the earth itself — belongs to God. We are not owners but stewards, entrusted with what is his and accountable for how we use it. Stewardship is not a financial category. It is a way of seeing the whole of life as held in trust for the One who gave it.

Acknowledging God's Ownership

The tithe is not a tax or a church fee — it is an acknowledgment. To return a tenth of our income to God is to declare publicly that it all belongs to him. This act of faith breaks the power of covetousness and opens our hands to the generosity that flows naturally from a heart that trusts God as Provider.

Malachi 3:10 "Bring the whole tithe into the storehouse, that there may be food in my house. Test me in this, says the LORD Almighty, and see if I will not throw open the floodgates of heaven and pour out so much blessing that there will not be room enough to store it."

Time and Ability

Stewardship extends beyond money. Our time, our skills, our health, our influence — all are gifts entrusted to us for purposes greater than our own comfort. The question a faithful steward asks is not "What can I keep?" but "What can I return?" Every hour, every talent, every opportunity is a trust.

1 Chronicles 29:14 "But who am I, and who are my people, that we should be able to give as generously as this? Everything comes from you, and we have given you only what comes from your hand."

Care for the Earth

At creation, God charged humanity with the stewardship of the earth — to tend and keep it. That mandate has not been revoked. Caring for the created world is not an optional extra for the environmentally minded; it is a responsibility that flows directly from acknowledging God as Creator and ourselves as his stewards.

Genesis 2:15 "The LORD God took the man and put him in the Garden of Eden to work it and take care of it."
