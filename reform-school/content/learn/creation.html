God did not discover the universe — He made it. In six days He created the heavens and the earth, rested on the seventh, and declared it all very good. Creation is not a backdrop to the story of salvation — it is the opening act, and it tells us everything about who God is and who we are.

An Act of Love

Creation was not an accident, a necessity, or an experiment. God created out of the overflow of his own loving nature — freely, intentionally, and with purpose. Every galaxy, every creature, every grain of sand exists because God willed it into being and declared it good. The world we inhabit is not a neutral space. It is a gift.

Genesis 1:31 "God saw all that he had made, and it was very good."

Six Days and the Sabbath

Scripture is specific: God created in six literal days and rested on the seventh. This is not poetic language for an indefinite process — it is the foundation of the weekly cycle that every human being still lives by. The Sabbath is embedded in the structure of creation itself, a weekly reminder that we belong to a Creator who is also our Redeemer.

Exodus 20:11 "For in six days the LORD made the heavens and the earth, the sea, and all that is in them, but he rested on the seventh day. Therefore the LORD blessed the Sabbath day and made it holy."

Created for Relationship

The crown of creation was not a mountain or an ocean — it was a man and a woman, made in the image of God, given dominion, and charged with care. We were made for relationship with God and with one another. The creation mandate is not obsolete — it still defines our purpose and our dignity.

Revelation 14:7 "Worship him who made the heavens, the earth, the sea and the springs of water."
