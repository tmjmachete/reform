The church is one body with many members — drawn from every nation, kindred, tongue, and people. In Christ, every barrier that divides humanity has been dismantled. Our unity is not uniformity; it is the deeper oneness that comes from belonging to the same Lord.

One in Christ

Distinctions of race, culture, nationality, class, and gender must not divide the people of God. In Christ we are a new creation — and the new creation does not operate by the broken hierarchies of the old. We are all equal before the cross, all equally loved, all equally needed. The ground at the foot of the cross is level.

Galatians 3:28 "There is neither Jew nor Gentile, neither slave nor free, nor is there male and female, for you are all one in Christ Jesus."

The Source of Unity

Our unity does not come from shared culture, shared language, or shared preferences. It comes from the oneness of the triune God who has adopted us as his children. We are united because we share the same Father, the same Saviour, the same Spirit, the same faith, and the same hope. This unity is a witness to the world of the power of the gospel.

John 17:21 "That all of them may be one, Father, just as you are in me and I am in you. May they also be in us so that the world may believe that you have sent me."

Called to Serve

Unity is not passive. It is expressed in mutual service — to serve and be served without partiality or reservation. The unity of the body is built as each member uses their gifts for the benefit of others, bearing one another's burdens, and carrying together what none could carry alone.

Ephesians 4:3 "Make every effort to keep the unity of the Spirit through the bond of peace."
