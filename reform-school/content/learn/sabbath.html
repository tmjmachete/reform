The Sabbath is the greatest outward sign of how we fear God — an acknowledgment of Him as both Creator and Redeemer.

Why the Sabbath?

Our weekly cycle of six days of work and rest on the seventh is not arbitrary. It is because of God. The Genesis account is a symbol of why we should fear him — he created it all, including you and me.

Genesis 1:1"In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth."

Creator and Redeemer

Exodus 20:8–10"Remember the Sabbath day, to keep it holy. Six days you shall labour... but the seventh day is a Sabbath to the LORD your God."

Verses 8-9 sound like an instruction from any human being. But verse 10 gives the reason — and the reason is God. By keeping the Sabbath we are acknowledging God not just as our Creator but as our Redeemer.

Deuteronomy 4:4–8The keeping of the commandments is a sign of the nearness of God to his people. What nation has God so near to them?
