We live in the hour of God's judgment. This is not a moment to fear — it is a moment to understand.

What Is Judgment?

There are three parts to any judgment: decision-making, opinion, and a legal system. The judgment of Revelation 14:7 involves all three — it is a decision-making, opinion-based legal system with a verdict: guilty or innocent.

A Case Study: The Fall

Genesis 3:6–9 (NKJV)"Then the Lord God called to Adam and said to him, 'Where are you?'"

God was not seeking Adam and Eve's location to chase them out. He was always in communion with them — it was their actions that changed the nature of his coming.

Education, p. 21"Often as they walked in the garden in the cool of the day they heard the voice of God, and face to face held communion with the Eternal."

Sin Separates

Isaiah 59:1–2 (NKJV)"But your iniquities have separated you from your God; and your sins have hidden His face from you, so that He will not hear."

Judgment would not have been necessary if they had not sinned. It is through our actions that we determine what the purpose of God's coming means for us.
