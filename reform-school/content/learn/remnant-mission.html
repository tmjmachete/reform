In the last days, a remnant has been called out — not to withdraw from the world, but to deliver the most urgent message it has ever heard. Three angels. Three declarations. One everlasting gospel.

The Remnant Defined

The universal church is composed of all who truly believe in Christ. But in the final chapter of history, a remnant emerges — a people who keep the commandments of God and hold to the faith of Jesus. This is not an exclusive club. It is a calling to every believer to have a personal part in a worldwide witness.

Revelation 12:17 "Then the dragon was enraged at the woman and went off to wage war against the rest of her offspring — those who keep God's commands and hold fast their testimony about Jesus."

The Three Angels' Message

At the centre of the remnant's mission is the proclamation of Revelation 14:6–12 — three angels flying in midair, each carrying a message of eternal consequence. Together they form the final gospel appeal before Christ's return.

The first angel calls the world to fear God, give Him glory, and worship the Creator — because the hour of His judgment has come. The second angel announces the fall of Babylon, the corruption of false religion. The third angel warns against the mark of the beast, calling God's people to patient endurance and faith.

Revelation 14:6–7 "Then I saw another angel flying in midair, and he had the eternal gospel to proclaim to those who live on the earth — to every nation, tribe, language and people. He said in a loud voice, 'Fear God and give him glory, because the hour of his judgment has come. Worship him who made the heavens, the earth, the sea and the springs of water.'"

Why This Matters Now

This proclamation coincides with Christ's work of investigative judgment in the heavenly sanctuary — a work that began in 1844 and is nearing its close. The remnant does not proclaim these messages as an obligation but as a privilege: to stand at the hinge of history and point people toward the one who can save them.
