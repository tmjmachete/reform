The gift of prophecy did not cease with the closing of the biblical canon. It is an identifying mark of the remnant church in the last days — a gift through which the Spirit continues to speak comfort, guidance, correction, and instruction to the people of God.

A Promised Gift

God promised through the prophet Joel that in the last days he would pour out his Spirit on all flesh, and sons and daughters would prophesy. This promise was partially fulfilled at Pentecost — and continues to be fulfilled in the life of the church. The Spirit does not fall silent when the need is greatest.

Joel 2:28–29 "And afterward, I will pour out my Spirit on all people. Your sons and daughters will prophesy, your old men will dream dreams, your young men will see visions. Even on my servants, both men and women, I will pour out my Spirit in those days."

The Spirit of Prophecy

Seventh-day Adventists believe the prophetic gift was manifested in the ministry of Ellen G. White, whose writings have brought comfort, guidance, and correction to the church. Her writings speak with prophetic authority — but they always point back to the Scripture as the ultimate standard by which all teaching and experience must be tested.

Revelation 19:10 "For it is the Spirit of prophecy who bears testimony to Jesus."

Scripture Remains Supreme

The prophetic gift, however it is expressed, never supersedes Scripture. The Bible remains the canon — the rule and measure by which all prophetic claims are judged. The gift of prophecy serves the Word; it does not replace it. Every vision, every impression, every word claiming prophetic authority must be weighed against what God has already spoken.

Isaiah 8:20 "Consult God's instruction and the testimony of warning. If anyone does not speak according to this word, they have no light of dawn."
