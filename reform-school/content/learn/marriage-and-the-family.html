Marriage was not invented by human society — it was established by God in Eden before sin entered the world. It is a lifelong covenant between a man and a woman, designed to reflect the love, sanctity, and permanence of the relationship between Christ and his church.

A Divine Institution

God himself performed the first marriage. He created woman from man, brought her to him, and declared them one flesh. This is not merely a social arrangement — it is a covenant with God as witness, built on mutual love, honour, and responsibility. What God has joined together, no human authority can legitimately dissolve.

Genesis 2:24 "That is why a man leaves his father and mother and is united to his wife, and they become one flesh."

A Reflection of Christ and the Church

Christian marriage is designed to be a living parable — the husband's love a reflection of Christ's self-giving love for the church, the wife's trust a reflection of the church's willing submission to Christ. This is not a hierarchy of worth but a picture of a relationship defined by love, service, and mutual honour.

Ephesians 5:25 "Husbands, love your wives, just as Christ loved the church and gave himself up for her."

The Family as a Witness

The family is the basic unit of society and the first school of faith. Parents are called to bring up their children to love and obey God — by example, by instruction, and by creating a home where the presence of God is real and the grace of Christ is experienced daily. A healthy family is one of the most powerful witnesses a church can offer.

Deuteronomy 6:6–7 "These commandments that I give you today are to be on your hearts. Impress them on your children. Talk about them when you sit at home and when you walk along the road, when you lie down and when you get up."
