The church is not a building, a denomination, or an institution. It is the community of all who confess Jesus Christ as Lord and Saviour — called out from the world, gathered together in his name, and sent back into the world with his gospel.

The Body of Christ

The church is the body of Christ — a living community of which he is the head. It is not a collection of individuals who happen to believe similar things. It is an organism, each member connected to the others, each needed, each gifted for the common good. No one grows in isolation. We were made for belonging.

Ephesians 1:22–23 "And God placed all things under his feet and appointed him to be head over everything for the church, which is his body, the fullness of him who fills everything in every way."

Gathered for Worship and Mission

The church gathers for worship, fellowship, instruction in the Word, and the celebration of the Lord's Supper. But it does not gather merely for itself. Every act of gathering sends us out again — equipped, encouraged, and commissioned to proclaim the gospel to every nation, tribe, language, and people.

Matthew 28:19–20 "Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptising them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, and teaching them to obey everything I have commanded you."

The Bride of Christ

The church is also called the bride of Christ — the people for whom he died, whom he is sanctifying, and whom he will present to himself at his return as a glorious church, holy and without blemish. The church's future is not decline but glory. History is moving toward a wedding, not a funeral.

Ephesians 5:25–27 "Christ loved the church and gave himself up for her to make her holy... to present her to himself as a radiant church, without stain or wrinkle or any other blemish, but holy and blameless."
