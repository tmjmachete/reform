Salvation is not a destination — it is a journey. The Christian life is a daily, deepening walk with a living Saviour who has not only forgiven our past but is actively at work shaping our future. Growth is not optional for the believer. It is the natural movement of a life rooted in Christ.

Freedom from the Past

Through the cross, Christ has triumphed over the forces of evil. The power that sin once held over us has been broken. We are no longer defined by what we have done — we are defined by what Christ has done for us. This is not a licence to continue in sin; it is the foundation from which genuine change becomes possible.

Galatians 5:1 "It is for freedom that Christ has set us free. Stand firm, then, and do not let yourselves be burdened again by a yoke of slavery."

Daily Communion with Christ

Growth requires connection. We grow as we commune with Christ daily — in prayer, in the Word, in worship, in community, and in service. These are not religious duties to fulfil; they are the means by which the relationship deepens and the character is shaped. A tree does not strain to grow — it simply stays rooted.

John 15:5 "I am the vine; you are the branches. If you remain in me and I in you, you will bear much fruit; apart from me you can do nothing."

Called to Serve

The Christian life is not inward only. We are called to follow Christ's example by ministering to the physical, emotional, and spiritual needs of those around us. As we give ourselves in loving service, the Spirit transforms every ordinary moment into a spiritual experience. Growth and witness are inseparable.

2 Peter 3:18 "But grow in the grace and knowledge of our Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ. To him be glory both now and forever!"
