# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Broader Christian community: church-going readers who want thoughtful, honest writing about faith and life. Many come from the re:form podcast audience; others discover the journal through search or sharing. They arrive in a reflective state, looking for something that names what they're experiencing, not a program to follow or a sermon to endure.

## Product Purpose

Reform Journal is the written companion to the re:form Podcast. It publishes long-form journal entries exploring faith, doubt, failure, and the ongoing work of finding your way back to God. Success is a reader who finishes an entry and feels less alone in their own story.

## Brand Personality

Honest, unhurried, human. The journal should feel like a trusted friend writing a real letter, not a ministry platform producing content. Voice is conversational and direct; never clinical or corporate. Warmth comes from candor, not coziness.

## Anti-references

- SaaS-style ministry tech: gradient cards, glass morphism, startup-clean layouts applied to faith content. This aesthetic signals "product launch," not "honest conversation."
- The generic Christian publishing look: stock-photo crosses, pastel palettes, decorative Scripture callouts, LifeWay-template energy. Visually safe in a way that feels hollow.

## Design Principles

1. **The writing is the hero.** Typography and layout exist to serve the text. Anything that competes with the prose is wrong.
2. **Print-earned warmth.** Analog references (letterpress, broadsheet, paper grain) work because they carry weight and permanence. Use them because they mean something, not as decoration.
3. **Conversation, not sermon.** Every layout decision should feel like it's pulling the reader closer, not positioning an authority above them. No pedestals.
4. **Honesty over gloss.** Rawness and restraint over slick production. If a design choice reads as "polished ministry brand," reconsider it.
5. **Faithful to the human first.** Serve the person reading, not the category of "Christian content." Resist every aesthetic shortcut that signals religion before it signals humanity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as baseline. Prioritize high-contrast body text for long-form reading. Respect reduced-motion preferences in any animation.
